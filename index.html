Design Philosophy

  1. Users first, design second

    Good design starts with actually understanding the person using it. Every layout, colour, and interaction should have a reason and that reason should always come back to making things clearer or easier for whoever's on the other end.

  2. Simple is harder than it looks

    The best interfaces feel effortless, but that never happens by accident. It takes a lot of iteration and even more cutting to get something down to only what it needs to be.

My Process

  1. 01 — Empathize

    Understanding the user — their needs, frustrations, and goals — before anything else.

  2. 02 — Define

    Turning research into a clear problem statement that keeps the design focused and intentional.

  3. 03 — Ideate

    Exploring solutions broadly before narrowing down to what actually works for the user and the brief.

  4. 04 — Prototype

    Building testable versions of the design — from rough wireframes to polished high-fidelity screens.

  5. 05 — Test

    Validating with real users, catching what doesn't work, and refining until it feels right.
